The season for skiing classic volcano descents on Mount Baker is just around the corner. With a descent of almost 8000 vertical feet, Mount Baker should be on everyone’s tick list. Join us in April and May for this classic ski descent!! Learn more

 

 WHAT'S HOT!

Avalanche Education and Backcountry Ski programs - The heart of winter has hit the northwest and the snow is flying in abundance. There is still time to get trained up on one of our AIARE avalanche level 1 programs or you can join us for one of our guided backcountry ski adventures! Winter courses wrap up in mid-March so the time is now to get out there and play in the snow! But, after that, let’s ski down some volcanoes! Learn more

Nun Peak Expedition- Join Mountain Madness in culturally rich Ladakh, India and break the 7,000 meter barrier! Nun Peak rises to 7,135 m / 23,409 feet and is the highest point of the remote Zanskar Range of the Western Himalaya. Led by Ossy Freire, this is a classic style expedition with easy access by road and a short approach to base camp. Buddhist culture, ancient monasteries and travel along the Silk Road combine for adventure travel at its most spectacular and a great introduction to big-peak expedition climbing, Madness style! Learn more

Ecuador Mountaineering School & Climbs - The mountains of Ecuador’s famous “Avenue of the Volcanoes” offer some of the finest mountaineering objectives in the Andes of South America and are set amid a fascinating cultural backdrop. Join our Mountaineering School or sign up to climb one or more summits. Learn more here!

OUR MISSION

Our mission at Mountain Madness is to provide the highest quality experience for beginner and advanced mountaineers and trekkers alike; exploring both the familiar and the wild, lesser-traveled regions of the world. Every trip we offer provides highly qualified guides (expert in their specific destination), the opportunity for cultural exchange, an extraordinary mental and physical challenge unique to mountain travel, wilderness skills development, self-discovery, and environmental awarness.

It’s not the destination that’s important; it’s how you get there...
